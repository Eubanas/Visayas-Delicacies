Lumpiang Ubod

Lumpiang Ubod is a fresh variety of Filipino lumpia, filled with palm heart or ubod, the edible pith from coconut or palm tree. The dish originated from Silay, a city in the province of Negros Occidental, where Lumpiang Silay, the original variant, is still prevalent. The lumpia has three components, the filling, the homemade wrapper, and the brown sauce.
