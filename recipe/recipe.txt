
Here are the recipes for Binakol, Dried Mangoes, Otap, Piaya, Pocherong Bisaya, Chicken Molo Soup, La Paz Batchoy, and Humba:

1. Binakol (Chicken Binakol)
Ingredients:

1 whole chicken (cut into pieces)
2 tablespoons vegetable oil
1 onion, chopped
4 cloves garlic, minced
2 medium-sized ginger, sliced
2 stalks lemongrass, crushed
1 can coconut milk
2 cups chicken broth
1 large green papaya, peeled and sliced into strips
1 cup malunggay (moringa) leaves (optional)
Salt and pepper to taste
Fish sauce (patis) to taste
Instructions:

Heat oil in a pot and sauté the garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned.
Add the coconut milk, chicken broth, lemongrass, and green papaya.
Bring to a boil, then simmer for about 30 minutes until the chicken is cooked and tender.
Season with salt, pepper, and fish sauce to taste.
Add the malunggay leaves last and cook for another 2 minutes.
Serve hot.
2. Dried Mangoes
Ingredients:

4 ripe mangoes (preferably Carabao variety)
Sugar (optional, for sweetening)
A pinch of salt
Instructions:

Peel the mangoes and remove the pit.
Slice the mangoes thinly.
Place the mango slices on a baking sheet and sprinkle a little sugar and salt (optional) to enhance flavor.
Dry the mango slices in a dehydrator or in an oven at a low temperature (about 135°F or 57°C) for 6-8 hours, or until they are leathery and pliable.
Allow them to cool and store in an airtight container.
3. Otap
Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour
1/2 cup margarine or butter
1/4 cup sugar
1 egg
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1/4 teaspoon salt
1 tablespoon cornstarch
1/2 cup powdered sugar (for dusting)
Instructions:

In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, cornstarch, sugar, and salt.
Add butter and vanilla, and mix until it forms a dough.
Roll the dough out thinly and cut into rectangles or your desired shape.
Brush with egg wash (beaten egg) on top.
Bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 15 minutes or until golden brown.
Allow the otap to cool and dust with powdered sugar before serving.
4. Piaya
Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour
1 tablespoon sugar
1 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup water (or more as needed)
1/4 cup vegetable oil
1 cup muscovado sugar (or brown sugar)
1/2 teaspoon sesame seeds (optional)
Instructions:

In a bowl, mix the flour, sugar, salt, water, and oil to form a dough. Knead until smooth.
For the filling, mix muscovado sugar and a pinch of salt together.
Roll the dough into small balls, flatten them, and place a small amount of the sugar filling in the center.
Pinch the edges to seal and roll it out flat again.
Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and cook the piaya until golden brown on both sides.
Optional: sprinkle with sesame seeds while cooking.
Let the piaya cool before serving.
5. Pocherong Bisaya
Ingredients:

1 kg pork (shoulder or belly), sliced
2 tablespoons vegetable oil
1 onion, chopped
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 medium-sized tomato, chopped
1 carrot, sliced
2 potatoes, peeled and sliced
1/2 head of cabbage, chopped
2 liters water
Salt and pepper to taste
Fish sauce (patis) to taste
Instructions:

In a large pot, heat oil and sauté the garlic, onion, and tomato until softened.
Add the pork slices and cook until browned.
Pour in water and bring it to a boil. Lower the heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes until the pork is tender.
Add the potatoes and carrots, and cook until soft.
Add the cabbage, season with salt, pepper, and fish sauce, and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes.
Serve hot.
6. Chicken Molo Soup
Ingredients:

1/2 kg ground chicken
1/2 cup minced shrimp (optional)
1/4 cup minced onion
2 tablespoons fish sauce
1 tablespoon cornstarch
2 tablespoons oil
1 clove garlic, minced
1 liter chicken broth
10 pieces wonton wrappers
Salt and pepper to taste
Green onions for garnish
Instructions:

In a bowl, combine the ground chicken, minced shrimp, onions, fish sauce, cornstarch, salt, and pepper.
Place a small spoonful of the filling in the center of each wonton wrapper, fold it, and seal the edges.
Heat oil in a pan, sauté the garlic, and add the chicken broth.
Bring the broth to a boil, then drop the dumplings in and cook for 10-15 minutes or until the dumplings are tender.
Garnish with green onions and serve hot.
7. La Paz Batchoy
Ingredients:

1 kg pork (preferably pork belly or ribs)
1/2 cup shrimp or fish stock
2 tablespoons soy sauce
1 onion, chopped
3 cloves garlic, minced
1/2 cup chicharrón (crispy pork skin), crushed
1/2 cup pork liver, chopped (optional)
4 cups beef broth
200 grams miki noodles (egg noodles)
Salt and pepper to taste
Spring onions, for garnish
Instructions:

Boil the pork in water for 30 minutes or until tender. Set aside the meat and broth.
In a pot, sauté garlic and onions, then add the pork, stock, soy sauce, and beef broth.
Bring to a boil, and then add the miki noodles.
Simmer for 10-15 minutes until the noodles are cooked and the broth has thickened slightly.
Add pork liver and chicharrón. Season with salt and pepper.
Garnish with spring onions and serve hot.
8. Humba
Ingredients:

1 kg pork belly, cut into chunks
1 onion, sliced
1 head garlic, minced
2 tablespoons soy sauce
1 tablespoon fish sauce
1 tablespoon vinegar
1 tablespoon brown sugar
1 cup pineapple chunks (with juice)
1 bay leaf
1/2 cup water
Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:

In a pot, sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant.
Add the pork belly pieces and cook until browned on all sides.
Add the soy sauce, fish sauce, vinegar, sugar, pineapple with juice, and bay leaf. Stir to combine.
Pour in the water and simmer for 40-60 minutes until the pork is tender and the sauce has reduced.
Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve hot.
